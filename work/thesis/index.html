Through Sick and Sane

This is an on-going thesis project towards the MArch Degree at the Graduate School of Design. Architecture has been used as a signifier for reality and human conditions since its inception (e.g. a prison signifies incarceration; a temple signifies worship; an asylum signifies attitude towards the mentally ill). However, Iâ€™d like to argue that architecture is not only a signifier / a proxy for reality, but it actually can be the primary agent in producing a para-realism that is simultaneously sick and sane. By designing the supreme court building, the architectural embodiment of supreme reason and judgment, I aim to produce an edifice and architectural type that is at once extremely honest and insane in its honesty.
